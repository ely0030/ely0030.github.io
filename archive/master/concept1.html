Key Concept: Fear-Based World

This is a cornerstone concept that has emerged from my essays and thinking. It represents a synthesized view that has remained stable through ongoing reflection.

The concept of a "fear-based world" describes how trauma can fundamentally alter one's perception of reality, creating a framework where everyday actions become associated with negative emotions and consequences. This concept builds on ideas from my essay on cPTSD, but represents a more developed framework.

Core elements of this concept include:

  • The pervasiveness of fear associations in mundane tasks
  • How this creates systematic avoidance patterns
  • The connection to apparent attention deficits and cognitive patterns

This concept helps explain broader psychological phenomena beyond individual experience.
